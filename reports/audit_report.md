## 3. Cooling and HVAC Systems

### Assessment

The facility's air conditioning units are routinely serviced, and thermostat settings are generally maintained within the recommended range of **22°C–24°C**. Doors and windows are typically kept closed during cooling operations, and building insulation appears adequate.

### Observations

Despite the generally good maintenance practices, the following issues were identified:

* Excessive cooling in some departments.
* Air leakage through doors, windows, or other building envelope gaps, leading to energy losses.

## 4. Equipment and Machinery

### Assessment

The audit revealed that some equipment remains powered on during periods of inactivity, resulting in avoidable energy consumption. Certain older and less energy-efficient machines are still in operation. Preventive maintenance schedules are generally followed, and motors and compressors appear to be reasonably optimized.

* Detected Standby Power Consumption: **(...) kW**

### Issues Identified

* Equipment left running during idle periods.
* Presence of aging equipment with lower energy efficiency.

---

## 5. Refrigeration Systems

### Assessment

The refrigeration units were evaluated for operational efficiency and maintenance condition.

### Observations

* Door seals were found to be in satisfactory condition.
* Some units were observed to be overfilled, restricting proper airflow.
* Frequent door opening was noted during operation.
* Ice accumulation was present in certain refrigeration units, reducing system efficiency.

### Issues Identified

* Reduced cooling efficiency due to overloading.
* Energy losses associated with excessive door opening.
* Increased compressor workload caused by ice buildup.

---

## 6. Digital and IT Infrastructure

### Assessment

The facility's IT infrastructure was reviewed to assess energy consumption patterns and operational practices.

### Observations

* Some devices are powered down after working hours.
* Energy-saving features have been enabled on selected devices.
* Certain unused devices continue to draw power while connected to the electrical supply.
* Opportunities exist to further optimize device power management.

### Issues Identified

* Standby energy consumption from unused devices.
* Inconsistent implementation of power-saving measures across departments.

---

## 7. Energy Behavior and Operational Practices

### Staff Behavior

Observations indicate that employees generally switch off lights when not required and turn off air conditioning units after working hours. Basic equipment shutdown procedures are in place.

### Operational Practices

The assessment identified the following gaps:

* No formal Energy Management Policy is currently implemented.
* Staff have not received structured training on energy efficiency and conservation practices.
* Energy consumption is not routinely monitored, analyzed, or reported.

### Issues Identified

* Lack of a structured energy management framework.
* Limited staff awareness and engagement in energy-saving initiatives.
* Insufficient monitoring and tracking of energy performance indicators.

---

## 8. Renewable Energy and Backup Systems

### Solar Photovoltaic (PV) System

#### Assessment

The facility has an installed solar PV system that contributes to meeting part of the site's electricity demand. However, the current installed capacity is insufficient to fully support the facility's energy requirements during normal operations.

### Issues Identified

* Solar PV capacity does not adequately meet total facility demand.
* Potential opportunities exist for system expansion and optimization.

---

## 9. Auditor's Summary

### Overall Energy Efficiency Rating

**Poor**

### Key Findings

* Significant opportunities exist to reduce lighting-related energy consumption.
* HVAC systems require optimization to eliminate excessive cooling and air leakage.
* Equipment shutdown practices should be strengthened to minimize idle and standby energy use.
* Refrigeration systems require operational improvements to enhance efficiency.
* Energy management practices, monitoring, and staff awareness programs should be established and formalized.
* Existing solar PV capacity should be reviewed for possible expansion.
